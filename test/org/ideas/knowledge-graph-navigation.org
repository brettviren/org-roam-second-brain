#+title: Navigating Knowledge Graphs with Semantic Proximity
#+filetags: :idea:

Most knowledge management tools treat documents as a flat bag of keywords.
A more powerful mental model treats them as nodes in a graph where edge weight
represents semantic proximity. Rather than searching for documents, you
navigate from your current context outward through conceptually nearby nodes.

* The Core Insight
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       test-idea-knowledge-graph-nav-insight-0001
:END:

When you are writing about topic A, the most valuable related notes are not
necessarily the ones that share keywords with A — they are the ones that share
concepts, assumptions, or problem framings with A. Embedding-based similarity
captures this conceptual proximity in a way that term frequency cannot.

The practical implication: a "similar notes" sidebar that updates as you type
can surface connections you would not have thought to search for explicitly.
This turns the note-taking tool into a thinking partner rather than a retrieval
system.

* Implementation Ideas

- Incremental embedding updates: recompute only changed sections on save
- Decay-weighted relevance: recent notes score higher for the same similarity
- Cluster detection: group notes by semantic neighborhood for visualization
- Cross-modal similarity: compare notes to calendar events or email threads

* Related Reading

Semantic similarity in information retrieval goes back to latent semantic
analysis in the 1990s, but modern dense retrieval using transformer-based
embeddings achieves far higher quality at practical corpus sizes.
